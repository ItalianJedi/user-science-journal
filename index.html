Project: Goal Experiment Summary Report

Summary Report

For my final entry in my User Science Journal, I am going to summarize my goal and content experiments using Google Analytics.

Set Up and Goal

We learned how to set up Google Analytics by watching the set up videos by Google. I had to watch them twice to get a good idea of what Google Analytics is and how to utilize them. Our instructor also provided a couple walk throughs to help us get started. After setting up we were tasked to set up a Goal. The Goal I set up was was to get people to watch a video on accessibility called What’s New in Web Accessibility which is about a new auditing tool called Lighthouse that Google put in Chrome. I thought it was a very interesting discussion and very useful to this blog and class I am in. I started sharing the link on Slack and random people on Facebook. I even found a Web Accessibility group on Facebook which I joined and shared the link. I didn’t get very many people to click on it but was happy when I saw a couple people achieve my goal.

Content Experiments and Google Optimize

Next we were to set up content experiments to see how we can change the page to get more people to complete the goal. I ended up doing two experiments. The first one failed during set up so I was unable to see it finish it. Basically, I just made a new blog post linking to a new accessibility video. The second experiment was setting up Google Optimize. This wasn’t a very fun experiment because during set up I just changed the wording of the headline. I was trying to upload some screen caps but I am keep getting errors. Basically, the original headline was What’s New in Web Accessibility (Google I/O ’18) while the variant just took out (Google I/O ’18). Nothing groundbreaking or particularly interesting but the only thing I can really think of for a duration goal. Looking back I should have kept asking people to look at the video so I have more data to analyze. According to reporting in Google Optimize, where I see that there were two sessions for three days running (Google recommends two weeks) and in the improvement overview, the variant had one session.

Thoughts

I didn’t get many hits which I don’t believe was the point of this exercise. What we were trying to accomplish is understanding Google Analytics and how to set up Goals and Content Experiments. This is very important to a web developer even if they don’t use Google Analytics on a day to day basis. I think this will be useful for me when I enter the workplace and if I were to use it on personal projects. Knowing that this page would most likely not get many views, I choose a goal that would be relevant to someone who is interested in User Accessibility. To make it easier for people to complete the goal, I choose a 5 minute mark for a successful completion. I know I wouldn’t get anyone to complete the goal if it was the full 20 unless someone came on here on their own because it is something they are really interested in it. And even if they were really interested in it, I am sure they will most likely have seen the video. If I could do this all over again, I would have used the other website I have which is a music blog. I think I will be able to get people to get to it and possibly get traffic without me begging them to come. This was really fun and I may even try it on my music blog when I get some free time. I would have also tried to promote this more so I get better data to analyze.

Project: Content Experiments

Last week we set up goals. My goal was to see if the user will watch a video on web accessibility for 5 minutes. I thought it was an interesting video on an accessibility tool called Lighthouse that was added to Google Chrome. According to Google Analytics, two users completed that goal.

This week we are to set up content experiments on our site. Content experiments are slight changes intended to have more people complete the goal. I tried to set up one experiment but was unable to get it set up correctly. Then our instructor showed us a different experiment to set up which seemed to work a lot better for me. Experiment 1: I made a new post with a different video than the Lighthouse one. This was also from the public archives and dealt with Web Accessibility. Accessibility Matters: Creating a Better Web I didn’t start this experiment due to an error I came across.

It looks like I was able to complete the experiment but my instructor gave us a different experiment to run instead. Experiment 2: Google Optimize! Google Optimize is a new A/B test that Google has created. It will eventually replace the old way of doing content experiments so it is important that we are used to doing these types of testing. With this experiment, instead of using the Accessibility Matters video, the variant I set up was just a slight change in the title of the post. Instead of “What’s New in Web Accessibility (Google I/O ’18)” I just used “What’s New in Web Accessibility.” Other than sending the user to another video, I wasn’t quite sure how to set up an experiment that would be really useful for analysis. Luckily, I believe the goal of these projects is to demonstrate that we are able to set up these content experiments. The duration of the goal that I set up was three days and it looks like it had two experiment sessions, which was more than I expected.

Project: Gooooaaaal! (and Funnel)

This week we are tasked to set up goals using Google Analytics. I thought the set up was fine it just took me a little bit of time to figure out what my goal was going to be. I used the tutorial from our class and from the Google Analytics tutorial on setting up goals and funnels.

My Goal

For this project I want to track how long people watch this video called What’s New In Web Accessibility. For roughly a half hour the presenter talks about automating web accessibility. I want to track that in my post and send it out to as many people as I can. The presentation is about a Google project about automating web accessibility called Lighthouse. This project tests for SEO, best practices, and accessibility and has been integrated in Chrome Dev Tools. The presenter goes through an interesting walk through of his auditing of a website using Lighthouse. The video itself is 37 minutes but I want to see how long people watch the first section about Lighthouse which is about 20 minutes. I do not believe that many people will watch the full 20 minutes but I do think there is enough interesting content where a user will watch the first 5 minutes. The second part is equally as import which is about the Accessibility Object Model, proposed as JavaScript API but for the purposes for this project I just want to focus on the first 5 minutes as pertaining to Lighthouse. The goal I set up is called Lighthouse – Goal Duration Test and I set the details to track the goal of a duration of 5 minutes. Because it was a duration goal, I was unable to create a funnel since those are just for destination goals. I also added an id to the link to track the events whenever someone clicked on the link as opposed to watched the video embedded on the page. I made the id of “lighthouse”. I added the link to a couple of Slack channels though my school and am planning to send the link to friends, family and other ways to generate traffic.

Just Add Analytics

Just Add Analytics

And so I did. This week we are to add Google Analytics to a website we are managing to collect data on how the users engage with the site. I, of course, decided to add Google Analytics to this journal here. Here are some questions and my best guesses to these questions.

Questions

  • Who is coming to my site? Where are they coming from? What is the Bounce Rate?
  • How did they get here?
  • What is my most popular page?

Best guess answers

  • I would suspect mid-20s to late-30s. Basically classmates and friends who I bugged. I think my Bounce Rate would be pretty high. According to one of the additional readings for this week, blogs usually have a pretty high bounce rate and most visitors usually just check out the latest post.
  • I would assume they arrived to my site from a link that was provided to them.
  • I think the most popular would be the latest post. In this case, the Adding Accessibility post.

Setting up Google Analytics

The set up was pretty straightforward and I didn’t run into any complications. It was a little hard for me to understand everything by watching those videos the first viewing. It made a lot more sense when we had the little breakthroughs. I read the supplemental reading, Web Analytics Demystified and rewatched the videos and it made a lot more sense to me. It will still take me a long time to be close to mastering Google Analytics but we need to start somewhere.

Results

Who is coming to my site? What is the Bounce Rate? I am not expecting much just yet. I can’t imagine a lot of traffic to this page without me sending people links, which is what I eventually did.

This is kind of what I expected. I only had 7 user, 6 of which were new. There were 10 sessions which averaged about 1.43 sessions per user. There were 15 pageviews and 1.5 pages per session. Finally, now I know how much my friends and classmates love me because I have an average of 1 minute per session and an 80% bounce rate. I am not too bummed about he Bounce Rate because it is a blog. I don’t have any data for age or gender. It kind of makes me wonder if I did something wrong. I enabled Demographics and Interest Reports so maybe I need to wait for that information to get collected or it could be due to how low my traffic is to the site.

All of my users are from the United States. One of the users I’ve asked to visit my page was from Holland but I think I remember him saying he was visiting the States.

If I dig a little further I can see that most of the users are from a few cities in Washington State and one city is “not set”. How did they get here?

According to the Acquisition Overview report, 5 of the 7 users were direct while 2 were social. This is due to a combination of texting my link to people and asking people on my program’s Slack page and messaging people on Facebook. I am not surprised by these results.

What is my most popular page?

Looking at the Behavior Overview report, aside from the main blog page, three pages each had one view: Usability Test, Evaluating Accessibility, and Adding Accessibility Video with Subtitles. I did notice from this report that there is a fourth page that is missing a title which I will go back and fix.

Conclusions

I have a long way to go to really evaluate Google Analytics on my page. This is a small piece of a much bigger puzzle. Through time I will understand how to collect data better but I will also need to keep asking individuals to visit my page and click around. I tried to come up with about five questions but I wasn’t quite sure what I could or wanted to collect. When I get a chance I may add some tags to these posts so this page may come up in a search. The post I have in mind is the one about Vinyl Me Please. Google Analytics isn’t as daunting when you start clicking around and understand what you are looking at. I had fun with this project.
