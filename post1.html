User Science Journal

Reflections on Accessbility and Analytics

Posted by Tony Graf on December 10, 2018

Summary Report

For my final entry in my User Science Journal, I am going to summarize my goal and content experiments using Google Analytics.

Set Up and Goal

We learned how to set up Google Analytics by watching the set up videos by Google. I had to watch them twice to get a good idea of what Google Analytics is and how to utilize them. Our instructor also provided a couple walk throughs to help us get started.

After setting up we were tasked to set up a Goal. The Goal I set up was was to get people to watch a video on accessibility called What’s New in Web Accessibility which is about a new auditing tool called Lighthouse that Google put in Chrome. I thought it was a very interesting discussion and very useful to this blog and class I am in. I started sharing the link on Slack and random people on Facebook. I even found a Web Accessibility group on Facebook which I joined and shared the link. I didn’t get very many people to click on it but was happy when I saw a couple people achieve my goal.

Content Experiments and Google Optimize

Next we were to set up content experiments to see how we can change the page to get more people to complete the goal. I ended up doing two experiments. The first one failed during set up so I was unable to see it finish it. Basically, I just made a new blog post linking to a new accessibility video. The second experiment was setting up Google Optimize. This wasn’t a very fun experiment because during set up I just changed the wording of the headline.

I was trying to upload some screen caps but I am keep getting errors. Basically, the original headline was What’s New in Web Accessibility (Google I/O ’18) while the variant just took out (Google I/O ’18). Nothing groundbreaking or particularly interesting but the only thing I can really think of for a duration goal. Looking back I should have kept asking people to look at the video so I have more data to analyze. According to reporting in Google Optimize, where I see that there were two sessions for three days running (Google recommends two weeks) and in the improvement overview, the variant had one session.

Thoughts

I didn’t get many hits which I don’t believe was the point of this exercise. What we were trying to accomplish is understanding Google Analytics and how to set up Goals and Content Experiments. This is very important to a web developer even if they don’t use Google Analytics on a day to day basis. I think this will be useful for me when I enter the workplace and if I were to use it on personal projects.

Knowing that this page would most likely not get many views, I choose a goal that would be relevant to someone who is interested in User Accessibility. To make it easier for people to complete the goal, I choose a 5 minute mark for a successful completion. I know I wouldn’t get anyone to complete the goal if it was the full 20 unless someone came on here on their own because it is something they are really interested in it. And even if they were really interested in it, I am sure they will most likely have seen the video.

If I could do this all over again, I would have used the other website I have which is a music blog. I think I will be able to get people to get to it and possibly get traffic without me begging them to come. This was really fun and I may even try it on my music blog when I get some free time. I would have also tried to promote this more so I get better data to analyze.
